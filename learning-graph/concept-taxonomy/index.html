    Concept Taxonomy

    Course: Introduction to American Sign Language (ASL) Total Categories: 12 Generated: 2025-12-29

    Taxonomy Categories

    1. FOUND - Foundation Concepts

    TaxonomyID: FOUND

    Description: Core foundational concepts about ASL, communication, and the basic parameters of sign language (handshape, movement, location, palm orientation, facial expression).

    Concepts: American Sign Language, Deaf Community, Deaf Culture, Visual Communication, Manual Communication, Handshape, Palm Orientation, Movement, Location, Facial Expression

    Estimated Count: ~10 concepts

    2. BASIC - Alphabet & Numbers

    TaxonomyID: BASIC

    Description: Fundamental building blocks including the ASL alphabet, fingerspelling techniques, numbers, and counting concepts.

    Concepts: Fingerspelling, ASL Alphabet, Letter groups, Numbers 1-100, Counting, Age Signs, Time Expressions

    Estimated Count: ~20 concepts

    3. CONV - Conversation Basics

    TaxonomyID: CONV

    Description: Essential conversational signs for greetings, basic interaction, politeness, question words, and common phrases used in everyday communication.

    Concepts: Hello, Goodbye, Please, Thank You, Yes, No, Maybe, Help, Finished, What, Who, Where, When, Why, How

    Estimated Count: ~20 concepts

    4. PRON - Pronouns & Reference

    TaxonomyID: PRON

    Description: Pronouns, demonstratives, and basic grammatical concepts for referring to people, objects, and spatial locations.

    Concepts: I, You, He/She, We, They, This, That, Here, There, Signing Space, Directionality, Indexing, Topicalization, Negation, Question Formation

    Estimated Count: ~15 concepts

    5. EMOT - Emotions & Feelings

    TaxonomyID: EMOT

    Description: Signs and expressions for communicating emotions, feelings, states of being, and emotional intensity.

    Concepts: Happy, Sad, Angry, Excited, Nervous, Scared, Calm, Surprised, Confused, Love, Like, Dislike, Emotion Intensity

    Estimated Count: ~20 concepts

    6. PEOP - People & Family

    TaxonomyID: PEOP

    Description: Signs for family members, relationships, and different types of people in one's life and community.

    Concepts: Family, Mother, Father, Sister, Brother, Grandparents, Aunt, Uncle, Baby, Friend, Teacher, Student, Boy, Girl

    Estimated Count: ~15 concepts

    7. FOOD - Food & Eating

    TaxonomyID: FOOD

    Description: Vocabulary related to food, meals, eating, drinking, and expressing food preferences.

    Concepts: Food, Eat, Drink, Hungry, Thirsty, Meals (Breakfast/Lunch/Dinner), Common foods (Apple, Pizza, Milk, Cookie, Ice Cream), Favorite, Delicious

    Estimated Count: ~20 concepts

    8. ANIM - Animals & Nature

    TaxonomyID: ANIM

    Description: Signs for animals, plants, natural elements, and the environment.

    Concepts: Animal, Dog, Cat, Bird, Fish, Horse, Bear, Butterfly, Tree, Flower, Sun, Rain, Nature, Environment

    Estimated Count: ~15 concepts

    9. DESC - Descriptive Words

    TaxonomyID: DESC

    Description: Colors, adjectives, and descriptive vocabulary for characterizing objects, people, and experiences.

    Concepts: Color names (Red, Blue, Yellow, Green), Size (Big, Small), Temperature (Hot, Cold), Speed (Fast, Slow), Quality (Good, Bad, Beautiful), Age (New, Old)

    Estimated Count: ~15 concepts

    10. VERB - Action Verbs

    TaxonomyID: VERB

    Description: Common action verbs and activities for describing what people do.

    Concepts: Play, Run, Jump, Walk, Sit, Stand, Sleep, Read, Write, Draw, Watch, Learn, Teach, Practice, Perform

    Estimated Count: ~15 concepts

    11. GRAM - Grammar & Expression

    TaxonomyID: GRAM

    Description: Grammatical structures, linguistic features, non-manual signals, facial grammar, and expressive elements of ASL.

    Concepts: Classifier, Role Shift, Time Marker, Subject-Verb-Object, Depicting Verb, Spatial Reference, Agreement Verb, Non-Manual Signals, Eyebrow movements, Head nods/shakes, Eye Gaze, Mouth Morphemes, Question Face, Statement Face

    Estimated Count: ~20 concepts

    12. CULT - Culture & Communication

    TaxonomyID: CULT

    Description: Deaf culture, history, communication etiquette, cultural institutions, and performance/expression in the Deaf community.

    Concepts: Deaf Identity, Deaf Pride, Gallaudet University, Sign Language Interpreter, Deaf School, Visual Alert Systems, Captioning, ASL Recognition, Deaf Art, Regional Sign Variation, Eye Contact, Getting Attention, Signing Clearly, Storytelling, Self-Expression

    Estimated Count: ~15 concepts

    Design Rationale

    • Balanced Distribution: No single category exceeds 10% of total concepts (20 concepts), ensuring even coverage
    • Pedagogical Progression: Categories flow from foundational (FOUND, BASIC) to conversational (CONV, PRON) to vocabulary (EMOT, PEOP, FOOD, ANIM, DESC, VERB) to advanced (GRAM, CULT)
    • Age-Appropriate: Categories align with grades 4-8 learning objectives and the 6-week course structure
    • Cultural Inclusion: CULT category emphasizes Deaf culture awareness alongside language learning
    • Grammar Integration: GRAM combines linguistic structure with expressive elements (facial grammar, non-manual signals)
    • Clear Boundaries: Each category has distinct membership criteria to avoid ambiguity
